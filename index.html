The Complete Process

How to Get a Truck Driving Job in Europe

6 real steps — from preparing your documents to your first day driving in Europe

01
📋

Prepare Your Documents

Gather your CE licence, passport, CPC card (or plan to get one), medical certificate, and experience letters. Use our free Document Checklist tool to get the exact list for your target country.

02
💼

Apply for Jobs

Apply directly to EU trucking companies and job portals — LinkedIn, Indeed, Trans.eu, EURES, and Jobs in Poland. You can also use a recruitment agency (paid service) to speed up the process.

03
🤝

Interview & Job Offer

Once shortlisted, you will have an online or phone interview. Employers want reliability and experience — not perfect English. If selected, you receive a Job Offer Letter — the most important document for your visa.

04
📄

Work Permit Application

With your Job Offer Letter, your employer submits a Work Permit application on your behalf. This takes 4–12 weeks depending on the country. Poland and Hungary are faster; Germany takes longer.

05
🏛️

Visa Approval

After Work Permit is approved, you apply for a Work Visa at the EU embassy in your country. Submit all documents, attend the appointment, and wait for visa approval — usually 2 to 4 weeks.

06
🚛

Travel & Start Driving

Fly to Europe, register your address, collect your Tachograph card, complete any CPC training if needed, and start your European truck driving career. Welcome to the road! 🇪🇺

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the most common questions about truck driving in Europe

How can I get a truck driving job in Europe?
You need a valid CE licence, a CPC card, a work visa for your target country, and a medical certificate. Exact requirements vary by country. Use our free Document Checklist Generator to get the complete list for your target country.
What is the average truck driver salary in Germany in 2026?
Truck drivers in Germany earn €2,600–€3,500 gross per month. Net salary after tax is typically €1,850–€2,500 depending on experience, truck type, and employer. Use our free EU Salary Calculator above for a more accurate estimate.
What is a CPC card and is it mandatory?
The CPC (Certificate of Professional Competence) is mandatory for all professional truck drivers working commercially in the EU. It requires 35 hours of periodic training every 5 years. Without it, you cannot legally drive a truck for hire in Europe.
Is my CE licence from outside Europe valid in EU countries?
CE licences from non-EU countries are generally not directly recognized. Most EU countries require you to convert your licence or pass a practical driving test. Rules differ by country — use our Licence Compatibility Checker for your specific situation.
Which EU country is easiest for international truck drivers to enter?
Poland and Hungary are generally the most accessible entry points. Both have active recruitment from international drivers, a large transport sector, and a simpler visa process than Germany or Netherlands. Many drivers use Poland as a stepping stone before moving to higher-paying countries.
How many hours can I drive per day in Europe?
Under EU tachograph rules: maximum 9 hours per day (extendable to 10 hours twice per week). After 4.5 hours of continuous driving you must take a 45-minute break. The weekly limit is 56 hours and two-week limit is 90 hours. Use our free Driving Hours Calculator to stay within legal limits.
What is the Europass CV format and do I need it?
The Europass CV is the standard CV format recognized across all 27 EU countries, created by the European Union. It is the most accepted format by government bodies, logistics companies, and recruitment agencies in Europe. Use our free CV Builder to create one in minutes.
